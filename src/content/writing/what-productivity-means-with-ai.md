# Build an AGENT That Actually Understands You

Most people use AI by starting from scratch every time.

New chat. New prompt. Same context explained again. Same goals repeated again. Same frustration when the model gives something generic, bloated, or slightly off.

That was the problem I kept running into too.

I did not want just another chatbot. I wanted a system that could actually work with me daily — something that could understand how I think, what I am trying to build, what I tend to forget, where I get distracted, how I like to plan, and when I need pushback instead of politeness.

That is where this question framework came from.

I started by asking a simple thing:
**What would an AI need to know about a person to become genuinely useful over time?**

Not just for answers.
For planning.
For prioritization.
For memory.
For reminders.
For decisions.
For growth.

The more I worked on it, the clearer it became that a good personal AI should understand at least a few layers well:

* who you are and what you are optimizing for
* your goals, projects, and tradeoffs
* your strengths, friction points, and patterns
* how you like to work
* how you want the system to speak, challenge, and adapt
* what it should remember, resurface, and act on later

That is what led to this prompt and its question set.

The goal is simple: give your GPT enough structured context that it can stop behaving like a generic assistant and start behaving more like a personalized operating layer.

A good system should not just "answer." It should:

* organize messy thoughts
* classify ideas, tasks, and resources
* remember important context
* help you prioritize better
* surface old but relevant information at the right time
* adapt to your patterns over time

This framework is my attempt to make that easier for anyone.

If you want to try it, take the prompt, answer it honestly — even roughly — and paste it into your GPT, Claude, or Gemini. You do not need perfect answers. You just need enough truth for the system to begin understanding you.
